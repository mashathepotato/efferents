How it works
  1. Point efferents at your repo with one efferents.yaml — your train/eval commands, the metric to move, and budget + approval guardrails.
  2. It frames a falsifiable hypothesis, writes an experiment plan, and runs bounded experiments on your own compute.
  3. It writes reviewed Markdown memos into a local lab journal — every result claim linked to a run id, metric, or code diff.
Why local-first
Your compute, your data. Experiments run via commands you define. Nothing leaves the machine except the LLM calls you opt into — the demo makes none.
Provenance by construction. Each claim resolves to a run_id, a metric file, a log, or a diff — not a vibe.
Reproducible memos. Plain Markdown + JSONL you can diff, grep, and check into a repo.
Budgeted & gated. Hard ceilings on GPU hours and LLM spend; plan-then-execute approval before anything runs.
